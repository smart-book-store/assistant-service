The key is to think not just about the student, but also about the other crucial users in a child's educational ecosystem: the parent and the teacher. I'll frame these using the standard agile format:
**"As a [user type], I want [an action/goal], so that [a benefit/outcome]."**

Here are the user stories, broken down by user type and theme.

---

### 1. The Student (The Primary User)

This is the most important user. The experience must be engaging, supportive, and fun.

#### **Theme: Core Learning & Getting Help**

* **As a student,** I want to be able to ask the tutor a question using my voice, **so that
  ** I can get help even if I don't know how to spell the words.
* **As a student,** I want to take a picture of my math homework problem, **so that
  ** the tutor can show me the steps to solve it, not just the answer.
* **As a student,
  ** I want the tutor to explain a topic (like "volcanoes" or "fractions") in a simple way with cartoons and examples, *
  *so that** I can understand it easily.
* **As a student,** I want to practice my spelling or math facts in a fun game, **so that
  ** I can get better without it feeling like a test.
* **As a student,
  ** when I get an answer wrong, I want the tutor to give me a gentle hint instead of just saying "try again," **so that
  ** I can figure it out myself and feel smart.

#### **Theme: Motivation & Engagement**

* **As a student,
  ** I want to choose a friendly character (like a robot, an animal, or a wizard) to be my learning buddy, **so that
  ** learning feels less lonely and more fun.
* **As a student,** I want to earn points, stars, or badges for completing lessons and trying hard, **so that
  ** I feel motivated to keep learning.
* **As a student,** I want to unlock new items to customize my learning buddy or background, **so that
  ** I have a goal to work towards.
* **As a student,
  ** I want the tutor to celebrate my progress with fun animations and positive messages like "You're on a roll!", **so
  that** I feel proud of what I've accomplished.

#### **Theme: Personalization & Accessibility**

* **As a student,
  ** I want the questions to get a little harder when I'm doing well and a little easier when I'm struggling, **so that
  ** I don't get bored or frustrated.
* **As a student,** I want to be able to have the text on the screen read out loud to me, **so that
  ** I can understand everything even if I'm not a strong reader yet.
* **As a student,** I want the tutor to remember what I've already learned, **so that
  ** it doesn't ask me the same easy questions over and over.
* **As a student,** I want to tell the tutor what I'm interested in (like dinosaurs or space), **so that
  ** it can give me reading passages or math problems about those topics.

### 2. The Parent (The Facilitator & Guardian)

Parents are often the purchasers and monitors of the tool. They need to see value, trust the platform, and feel in control.

#### **Theme: Monitoring & Insight**

* **As a parent,** I want to see a simple dashboard showing my child's progress, **so that
  ** I can understand what they are learning and where they might be struggling.
* **As a parent,** I want to receive a weekly email summary of my childâ€™s activity and achievements, **so that
  ** I can stay informed without having to log in every day.
* **As a parent,
  ** I want the tutor to highlight specific skills my child has mastered and areas where they need more practice, **so
  that** I know how to best support them.

#### **Theme: Control & Safety**

* **As a parent,** I want to be able to set daily time limits or a schedule for using the tutor, **so that
  ** I can manage my child's screen time effectively.
* **As a parent,** I want to know the platform is a safe, ad-free environment with no external links or chat functions,
  **so that** I can have peace of mind while my child is learning.
* **As a parent,** I want to be able to select or restrict the subjects my child can access, **so that
  ** they can focus on school-related topics.

---

### 3. The Teacher (The Professional Integrator)

Teachers can use the AI tutor to supplement classroom instruction and provide differentiated support.

#### **Theme: Classroom Management & Integration**

* **As a teacher,** I want to be able to create student accounts for my entire class easily, **so that
  ** I can get them started without a lot of setup time.
* **As a teacher,** I want to assign specific topics or practice modules that align with my current lesson plan, **so
  that** students can get targeted, independent practice.
* **As a teacher,** I want the AI tutor to work well on shared devices like classroom tablets, **so that
  ** I can use it effectively in learning centers or for group rotations.

#### **Theme: Data & Differentiation**

* **As a teacher,** I want to see a class-level report that identifies common misconceptions on a topic, **so that
  ** I can re-teach that concept to the whole group.
* **As a teacher,** I want the system to automatically flag students who are falling significantly behind on a skill, *
  *so that** I can provide them with targeted, in-person support.
* **As a teacher,** I want to view an individual student's learning path and progress, **so that
  ** I can have more informed conversations during parent-teacher conferences.

By building features that address these user stories, an AI tutor can become a truly effective, engaging, and trusted educational tool for primary school students and the adults who support them.
